Tasting Order

Flight 1: Sparkling Introduction

SANTA LUCIA Franciacorta Brut 2021
Begin with this elegant sparkling wine to cleanse the palate

Flight 2: White Wines (Light to Full)

1. IDDA Etna Bianco 2023
Start with the mineral-driven Carricante from volcanic soils

2. QUINTODECIMO Fiano 2022
Move to this elegant and aromatic Campanian white

3. QUINTODECIMO GRECO DI TUFO 2022
Finish the whites with the most structured and powerful example

Flight 3: Lighter Reds

1. MALVIRA Mombeltramo Riserva 2009
Begin reds with this mature Nebbiolo from Roero

2. RIVETTO Barbaresco Marcarini 2016
Progress to this classic Barbaresco from an excellent vintage

Flight 4: Fuller-Bodied Reds

1. VAL DI SUGA Brunello 2018
Experience the classic Brunello expression first

2. VAL DI SUGA Brunello Spuntali 2018
Finish with the most powerful and complex wine of the tasting

Why This Order?

This progression follows the classic tasting principles of sparkling first, whites before reds, and increasing body and structure throughout each flight. The sequencing showcases each wine in its best light while preparing your palate for the next wine.

Sparkling Wine

SANTA LUCIA Franciacorta Brut 2021

$35
Lombardy | 90% Chardonnay, 10% Pinot Bianco
Tasting Notes

Yellow with greenish nuances, fine bubbles. Aromatic floral nose with a soft, slightly savory palate and pleasantly bitter finish.

Producer

Family-owned winery founded in the 1990s with 30 hectares of organically managed vineyards across the Franciacorta region.

White Wines

QUINTODECIMO Fiano 2022

$54
Campania (Avellino) | 100% Fiano
Tasting Notes

Bright straw yellow. Complex aromas of linden, acacia, thyme, exotic fruits, and candied citrus. Elegant, powerful and balanced with a long mineral finish.

Producer

Established in 2001 by Professor Luigi Moio, who studied enology in Burgundy and specializes in Campanian varieties.

QUINTODECIMO GRECO DI TUFO 2022

$54
Campania (Tufo) | 100% Greco
Tasting Notes

Golden yellow color reminiscent of Van Gogh's sunflowers. Aromas of quince, melon, apricot, cedar, and herbs. Structured like a red wine with remarkable balance.

Producer

"Giallo d'Arles" references Van Gogh's favorite yellow – fitting as Greco naturally has higher pigmentation than other white grapes.

IDDA Etna Bianco 2023

$69
Sicily (Mount Etna) | 100% Carricante
Tasting Notes

Bright straw yellow. Pure minerality with citrus, herbs, mint and jasmine. Firm texture with vibrant acidity and a flinty finish.

Producer

A collaboration between the Gaja (Piedmont) and Graci (Sicily) families. "IDDA" means "she" in Sicilian, referring to Mount Etna.

Red Wines

MALVIRA Mombeltramo Riserva 2009

$50
Piedmont (Roero) | 100% Nebbiolo
Tasting Notes

Ruby red with orange hues. Complex aromas of black fruit, chocolate, spices, and mint. Powerful with persistent flavors and distinctive spice and licorice notes.

Producer

Established in 1950 by Giuseppe Damonte, now run by his sons. The vineyard was originally owned by their grandmother Margherita.

RIVETTO Barbaresco Marcarini 2016

$69
Piedmont (Barbaresco) | 100% Nebbiolo
Tasting Notes

Bright garnet red. Classic Nebbiolo aromas of red berries, roses, violets, and leather. Elegant structure with cherry, raspberry, cinnamon and thyme flavors.

Producer

Four generations of winemaking since the late 19th century. Enrico Rivetto pioneered biodynamic viticulture, becoming the first Demeter-certified producer in Barolo/Barbaresco.

VAL DI SUGA Brunello 2018

$71
Tuscany (Montalcino) | 100% Sangiovese
Tasting Notes

Bright red with ruby highlights. Complex nose of ripe red fruit, orange, and balsamic notes. Well-structured with defined tannins and acidity. Persistent finish.

Producer

Unique for owning vineyards on all three slopes best suited to top-quality Brunello production—northeastern, southeastern, and southwestern.

VAL DI SUGA Brunello Spuntali 2018

$99
Tuscany (Montalcino - Southwest) | 100% Sangiovese
Tasting Notes

Deep ruby-garnet. Ripe candied fruit, spices, tobacco, herbs, and cocoa. Luxuriant profile with creamy texture and supple tannins, balanced by mineral and iodine notes.

Vineyard

15-hectare vineyard at 300m elevation with sandy, well-draining soils. Strong Mediterranean influence from the nearby Tyrrhenian Sea.

Serving Suggestions

Temperature

Franciacorta: 8-10°C (46-50°F)
White wines: 10-12°C (50-54°F)
Red wines: 16-18°C (61-64°F)

Decanting

Young whites: 15-30 minutes in glass
Aged whites: 15-30 minutes in glass
Malvira (2009): 30-60 minutes
Barbaresco/Brunello: 1-2 hours

Food Pairings

Franciacorta: Appetizers, seafood, light pasta
Fiano: Grilled fish, shellfish, fresh cheese
Greco: Rich seafood, white meats, aged cheese
Etna Bianco: Seafood with citrus, herb dishes
Mombeltramo: Game, roasted meats, aged cheese
Barbaresco: Truffle dishes, mushroom risotto
Brunello: Grilled/roasted meats, wild boar
Brunello Spuntali: Prime beef, game, complex dishes
